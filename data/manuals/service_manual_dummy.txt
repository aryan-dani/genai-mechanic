SECTION: ENGINE PERFORMANCE

DTC P0300 - Random/Multiple Cylinder Misfire Detected
Description:
A random or multiple cylinder misfire has been detected. This indicates that the engine is not burning fuel efficiently in multiple cylinders.

Possible Causes:
1. Ignition System: Faulty spark plugs, ignition coils, or ignition cables.
2. Fuel System: Low fuel pressure, clogged fuel injectors, or contaminated fuel.
3. Intake System: Vacuum leaks (intake manifold gasket, vacuum hoses, PCV valve).
4. Mechanical: Low engine compression, timing belt/chain skipped.

Diagnostic Steps:
1. Check for other DTCs. If P0171 (System Lean) is present, suspect a vacuum leak.
2. Inspect Vacuum Hoses: Check for hissing sounds or cracked hoses. Smoke test recommended.
3. Inspect Spark Plugs: Remove and check for wear, fouling, or incorrect gap.
4. Check Fuel Pressure: Verify pressure is within 45-55 PSI range.

Repair Procedures:
- Vacuum Leak Repair: Locate leak using smoke machine. Remove intake manifold if gasket is leaking. Clean surfaces. Install new gasket (Part #99-123). Torque bolts to 18 ft-lbs in star pattern.
- Spark Plug Replacement: Remove ignition coils. Remove old plugs. Install new plugs (Gap 0.044"). Torque to 12 ft-lbs.

DTC P0171 - System Too Lean (Bank 1)
Description:
The oxygen sensor detected that the air-fuel mixture is too lean (too much air, not enough fuel).

Possible Causes:
1. Vacuum leaks (most common).
2. Weak fuel pump.
3. Dirty Mass Air Flow (MAF) sensor.
